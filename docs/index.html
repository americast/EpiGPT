EpiGPT
Prompting LLMs for Epidemiology
About EpiGPT
Our goal is to investigate the feasibility of prompting Large Language Models (LLMs) like ChatGPT to predict the trajectory of an epidemic and generate useful information from the same. This means that instead of training the model, we provide it with prompts related to the epidemic and then query it for future predictions. Moreover, we are keen to understand how these models can be used for providing better natural language explanation to such time series data, such that they maybe easily understood by health experts and policy makers. We also look at the model's capability in simulating counterfactual scenarios, such as how the epidemic trajectory would change if a lockdown was to be declared.
Some results
We present some MAE values here:
Dataset EpiGPT ARIMA
CDC-Flu 9.23 7.96
CDC-Covid 19.76 25.89
Symptoms 0.49 0.32
Monash Covid 1.61 1.09
Monash Hospitalization 8.46 6.73

Thus EpiGPT gives at par or better results compared to ARIMA, even though there is no training involved and it is zero-shot.
